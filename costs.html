Supporting Users is Expensive

Traditionally, organisations rely on expensive telephone and email support, CRM services like Zendesk that still require a large body of support staff, or uncontrolled, inactive and unreliable community forums. How-tos, help manuals and guides are centrally produced and distributed at great expense, despite this knowedge existing in the user community.

The cost to the user of dedicated support means that many users are unwilling or unable to pay. Low margin products and startups with developing infrastructures are often unable to provide dedicated user support due to the high cost.

The average profit margin for SaaS companies is only 1.3%. Reductions in the cost of providing support services can mean the difference between a profit and loss, especially for new or niche software providers.1

1http://cloudstrategies.biz/imperative-reduce-cost-saas-service

Support Costs

Software companies typically spend as much as 20% of their revenues and 15% of their headcount providing support services. Yet the average support employee only processes around 100 calls and 30 e-mails a month, equal to less than six customer transactions per working day.1
