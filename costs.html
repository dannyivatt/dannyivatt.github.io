Supporting Users is Expensive

Traditionally, organisations rely on expensive telephone and email support, uncontrolled inactive and unreliable community forums or old fashioned centrally created documentation, how tos, help and guides.

The cost to the user of dedicated support means that many users are unwilling or unable to pay. Low margin products and startups with developing infrastructures are often unable to provide dedicated user support due to the high cost.

Support Costs

Software companies typically spend as much as 20% of their revenues and 15% of their headcount providing support services. Yet the average support employee only processes around 100 calls and 30 e-mails a month, equal to less than six customer transactions per working day.1

1 Association of Support Professionals, Maintenance & Services Ratios, 2014.

Development Costs
